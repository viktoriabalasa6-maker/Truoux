Compliance & Licensing

Regulated. Audited. Trusted.

Truoux treats compliance not as a checkbox but as a core competitive advantage — and a promise to every user on the platform.

🏦
US FinCEN MSB License
Issued by the US Treasury's Financial Crimes Enforcement Network. Our MSB registration authorizes virtual currency exchange and transfer services with full AML/KYC compliance. Registration is publicly verifiable and covers customer identity verification, transaction monitoring, suspicious activity reporting, and fund flow records.
🏛️
SEC Regulation D Qualification
Obtained from the US Securities and Exchange Commission in September 2025. This Regulation D exemption qualification authorizes Truoux to legally offer digital asset products within a strict legal and regulatory framework — signaling our highest standard of institutional compliance and transparency to global users.
🔍
AML / CTF Framework
Comprehensive Anti-Money Laundering and Counter-Terrorist Financing compliance: real-name KYC with address verification, transaction behavior monitoring, high-risk address identification, Suspicious Transaction Reports (STRs), on-chain behavior analysis, and real-time sanction list synchronization with third-party compliance vendors.
🛡️
Hacken Security Audit
Truoux's infrastructure, smart contracts, and platform security model were rigorously assessed by Hacken — one of the blockchain security industry's most respected audit firms. All critical findings were resolved prior to certification.
🔒
SlowMist Verification
SlowMist conducted an independent security review covering penetration testing, code auditing, and threat modeling. The dual-audit approach with both Hacken and SlowMist provides an unmatched level of security assurance for user funds and platform data.
📊
GDPR & CCPA Compliance
Truoux follows GDPR and CCPA international data compliance standards with minimized data collection, end-to-end encrypted transmission, user data export options, and granular privacy controls — ensuring users' rights to know, control, and delete their personal data.

FAQ — About

Questions About Truoux

When and where was Truoux founded?
+
Truoux was formally established in April 2019 in the United States. The founding team brings together specialists from fintech, blockchain technology, and trading systems. The company launched its beta platform in July 2019, reaching 200,000 registrations within weeks of launch.
What makes Truoux different from other exchanges?
+
Truoux was specifically designed to address the pain points common in mainstream platforms: high barriers to entry, complex processes, weak language localization, and lack of educational support. We built simplified registration, visual guided onboarding, multi-language interfaces, and a progressive growth system — all within a fully licensed, audited compliance framework.
Is Truoux publicly verifiable for compliance?
+
Yes. Our US FinCEN MSB registration is publicly verifiable through FinCEN's official database. Our SEC Regulation D qualification is filed with the Securities and Exchange Commission. Security audit reports from Hacken and SlowMist are available upon request. We are committed to full transparency in our regulatory standing.
How does Truoux support new traders?
+
Our platform combines graphical operation paths, distributed onboarding guidance, and cognitive-driven models to reduce the learning curve. The growth system maps user behavior to a progression path — from beginner to advanced — with educational content, community engagement, and tiered trading tools that scale with your experience.

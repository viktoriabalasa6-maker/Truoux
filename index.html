What licenses does Truoux hold?
+
Truoux holds two key US regulatory licenses: a Money Services Business (MSB) license issued by FinCEN (US Treasury), and a Regulation D exemption qualification from the US Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC), obtained in September 2025. We also maintain full AML/CTF compliance and underwent dual security audits by Hacken and SlowMist.
How are my assets protected on Truoux?
+
Over 95% of user funds are stored in multi-signature cold wallets, completely offline and protected from online threats. The remaining operational hot wallet funds are continuously monitored by our AI behavioral detection system. We also maintain cross-region disaster recovery backups with minute-level snapshot rollback capability.
Which countries and regions does Truoux support?
+
Truoux serves users in over 200 countries and regions with multi-language interface support. We have established localized operations in key emerging markets across Asia, South America, and beyond. Certain features or services may be restricted in specific jurisdictions — please consult our Terms of Service for regional details.
What trading products are available?
+
Truoux offers spot trading, derivatives (futures and contracts), asset management products, and strategy tools via our API. Our matching engine supports parallel order processing with millisecond-level execution, suitable for both retail traders and institutional algorithmic strategies.
Does Truoux offer an API for developers?
+
Yes. We provide standardized REST and WebSocket APIs covering market data subscription, order management, and account data. All endpoints are protected by permission verification and rate controls. Developers and institutional users can access custom data push and pull services for building automated trading systems.
How do I get started on Truoux?
+
Registration takes under 2 minutes — simply sign up with your email, complete our streamlined KYC identity verification, deposit funds, and you're ready to trade. Our visual onboarding guide and graphical operation flows are designed to get first-time users comfortable with the platform as quickly as possible.
